FAQ

+ - What oilseeds do you process?

We are oilseed agnostic, requiring only 20% oil content in the seed to make processing worthwhile.

+ - Why don’t farmers already process their own oilseed to meet their fuel needs? 

To expect every farmer to buy, install and tune ALL the equipment, have the time & expertise to make fuel that meets engine standards whilst developing a market for the protein rich meal, and disposing of the glycerin by-product, and do it year-in-year-out, all on their own, whilst also improving the technology, is unreasonable.

+ - Why bother? 

T80% of on-farm energy comes from diesel.  Australia imports most of it’s diesel, with the last 2 crude refineries receiving $2Billion to secure their profits, due to shut in 2030, leaving us 100% dependent on imports.  Food security depends on fuel security.  15% of vegetable oil currently goes into biodiesel, yet our farmers are still using fossil-diesel.  There are vertical integration economic gains to be made. Farmers need an option for harvests that are out of spec. To continue to use crude oil that props up regimes that murder journalists, funds terrorists, and bomb hospitals whilst invading their neighbors, just has to stop.

+ - Shouldn’t farmers focus of growing food not fuel? 

40% of all grains are used for livestock feed.  Livestock benefit from protein and carbohydrates, needing only a little oil. Ruminants can only cope with 5% oil in their diet, mono-gastrids 10%; Any more and their gut biome is disturbed. Oilseeds start at 20% oil, and hold more protein than conventional grains (wheat, barley, sorghum).  Stock-feed manufacturers only pay for the protein in a harvest, they don’t value or pay for the oil, combining it with the carbs into a figure of “digestible energy”.  We shouldn't be putting vegetable oil into our livestock. It's more valuable going into our steel horses. 

Fiber crops like Cotton, Flax, and Hemp, are also major sources of oilseeds, rich in oil and protein.
